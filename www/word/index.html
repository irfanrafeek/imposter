How to Play

A party game of bluff and wordplay — also known as Undercover

The Impostor Word Game (also called the Imposter Word Game or Undercover) is a party game where everyone secretly sees the same word — except one impostor, who only gets a vague hint. Players take turns saying one clue word each; the group tries to expose the impostor while the impostor bluffs and tries to guess the secret word. You can play it free online right here at impostorgames.com/word — no app, no sign-up, 3–20 players, works on any phone browser.

  1. 1

    Gather your group

    Three to twenty friends, each with their own phone, in the same room. Open this page on every device.

  2. 2

    Create or join a room

    One person taps Create and shares the 4-character room code or QR code. Everyone else taps Join.

  3. 3

    Draw your cards

    The host picks a category — Food, Animals, Places, Movies & TV, Football and more. When the round starts, everyone secretly sees the same word on their phone… except the impostor, who only sees a vague hint.

  4. 4

    Give clues

    Take turns out loud: each player says exactly one word related to the secret word. The impostor has to bluff along using just the hint.

  5. 5

    Accuse and defend

    Whose clue felt off? Discuss, accuse, defend — the group tries to agree on who the impostor is, while the impostor tries to work out the secret word from everyone's clues.

  6. 6

    The reveal

    The round ends when the group agrees on a suspect — or the impostor blurts out the secret word first. The host taps Reveal and every screen shows who the impostor really was.

FAQ

What is the Impostor Word Game?

It's a free online party game for 3–20 players, also known as Undercover or the Imposter Word Game. Everyone secretly sees the same word on their phone — except one impostor, who only sees a vague hint. Players take turns saying one clue word each; the group tries to expose the impostor, and the impostor tries to blend in and guess the secret word.

How do you catch the impostor?

Listen to everyone's clue words. Players who know the secret word give clues that clearly relate to it — the impostor has to bluff from just a hint, so their clues tend to be vague or slightly off. Discuss, accuse, and when the group has decided, the host taps Reveal.

Is there an Imposter Word Game app?

No download needed — the Impostor Word Game runs right in your phone's browser. Just open the page and share a room code. It works on iPhone and Android instantly, with no app to install.

Is it free to play?

Yes, completely free. There's no sign-up, no app to install, and no payment. Just open the page in any phone browser and share a room code with your friends.

How many players do I need?

At least 3 players, up to 20. One person creates the room and becomes the host; everyone else joins with the 4-character room code or by scanning the QR code.

What word categories are there?

Six at launch — Food, Animals, Places, Everyday Objects, Movies & TV, and Football — each with dozens of words. The host picks a category before every round, and each word comes with a matching vague hint for the impostor.

Can the impostor win?

Yes — two ways. Stay undetected until the end of the round, or work out the secret word from everyone's clues and say it out loud before being exposed.

Can I play on one device?

Each player should use their own phone so everyone sees their card privately — that's what keeps the impostor secret. You do all the clue-giving and accusing out loud in the same room.
